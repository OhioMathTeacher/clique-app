Overview

    NYT Connections has taken the puzzle world by storm, offering players a satisfying blend of logic, pattern recognition, lateral thinking, and clever misdirection. 

    The daily challenge tasks players with grouping 16 words into four connected categories: a deceptively simple concept that quickly escalates into a mental workout.

    Suppose you’ve ever wished you could create your own version of this viral puzzle, to entertain friends, test students, challenge followers, or simply explore your creativity. In that case, you’re about to learn how easy and enjoyable the process can be.

NYT Connections is one of the finest NYT word puzzles with millions of active players. The primary factor for the popularity involves four carefully curated categories, each containing four words that share a meaningful connection. While the first two categories are generally easy to guess, the third one is a little complicated, and finally, the last one presents the toughest challenge. 

If you are a hardcore NYT Connections fan, you can build your own Connections puzzle. The process doesn’t require any technical knowledge or coding experience. All you need are four categories, a set of 16 words, and a bit of imagination. Once you are accustomed to how the difficulty level increases at every step, crafting a puzzle that feels as polished as the official game becomes simple.

The magic of NYT Connections lies in the choice of words. When a fit occurs in more than one group, it makes the puzzle troublesome. Since you are making your own version, you’re controlling the difficulty, creativity and theme. 
Why Should You Make Your Own NYT Connections Game?

Before designing the puzzle, you should understand why the game works so well, and why creating your own version is both fun and rewarding. First, the structure is engaging. Players have to examine 16 words and search for connections between them. Sometimes the connections are obvious, and other times they’re well-hidden. Therefore, crafting your own puzzle will allow you to: 
Explore Creativity

You can choose categories that go from simple to deeply thematic ones. For example, it can be animals, celebrities, books, or space-related themes. 
Personalize for Specific Audiences

Connections can be used for teaching purposes. Teachers can use Connections for vocabulary or subject-based puzzles. Brands can create their own puzzle that focuses on marketing. Even game nights can have a Connections-like puzzle to keep them engaging. 
Control the Difficulty

Just like Connections’ yellow, green, blue, and purple tiers, you can assign any four colours of your choice to indicate difficult levels like Easy, Medium, Tricky, and Hard. However, remember that the difficulty of the puzzle can’t be determined solely by the theme, but also by the choice of words. 

Before you dive into creating your own Connections puzzle, ensure you have categories that are completely different from each other and don’t get repeated. Overlapping themes can confuse players in ways that feel unfair rather than challenging.

How to Make Your Own NYT Connections Game?

Creating your own custom puzzle is easier than you might think. It requires no technical knowledge. Using tools like connections.swellgarfo.com and ConnectionsPlus.io/create, one can easily design, shuffle and test grids. Below are the steps to design a Connections-like puzzle:
Choose Your Four Categories

First, one should choose four different themes. Ensure that you mix the difficulty levels to keep players engaged. For example, the Easy category can include themes such as types of bread or a specific kind of bird. Then, as they progress, the themes will become increasingly complex, ultimately culminating in space-related or book-related themes for the most challenging part. 
Pick Four Words for Each Category

Choose four words that fit a category. Choose difficult words. For example, if it’s a type of bread, you can go for Sourdough, Baguette, Pita, or Focaccia. Similarly, if it’s related to magic, take words like Wand, Hat, Rings, and Cards. 

While doing that, you have to ensure your words aren’t fitting exactly in two different categories. You can do that if you want to intentionally mislead gamers. 
Add Intentional Ambiguity

If you have noticed, NYT Connections often features one or two cleverly selected words to create confusion. For example, 'lime' can be either a fruit or a color shade. If both categories exist in the same puzzle, it will be confusing for players. 
Shuffle the 16 Words Randomly

Next, you have to shuffle the 16 words randomly, using tools like connections.swellgarfo.com or ConnectionsPlus.io/. These tools make sure that your words appear in a mixed grid. There must be a 4x4 grid, a simple list, and a graphic template. 

Once the puzzle is complete, you can directly go to ConnectionsPlus.io/create to check the difficulty of the puzzle in real time. Now, after the playtest, share it with friends. If they solve it instantly, the puzzle requires complexity. However, if they get stuck, simplify the puzzle a bit. Your aim should be to create a puzzle that’s clever, challenging, and satisfying.
